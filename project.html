PROBLEM

Wedding websites today help users with research, not planning. They lack the robust tools couples need to organize their event, don’t allow for collaboration and have very traditional branding.

SOLUTION

Gather brings modern couples’ favorite planning tools into one space where both partners will want to spend time planning together.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

Some of the bigger players in the wedding space are focused on blog content, vendor reviews or selling goods. While many do offer basic planning tools, such as wedding checklists, budgeting tools, and guest list managers, these tools aren’t collaborative. Need to plan something outside of these specific use cases? You can’t. In addition, their UIs are ad-heavy due to their focus on vendors. Through our analysis, we identified a gap in the market for a more collaborative, customizable, and modern wedding planning destination.

USER RESEARCH

To test our theory, we surveyed people about their wedding planning habits. Our findings supported the hypothesis that couples needed a more robust and collaborative planning product than what exists today:

63%

of people surveyed use more than one tool in parallel to plan wedding and wedding-related projects, such as honeymoons and shower. The most popular tools used are Google Drive and Pinterest.

80%

USER STORIES & FLOWS

Instead of the wedding-specific tools that other competitors already offer, we focused our user stories on the areas where our research showed users would benefit most: productivity-style tools like those they already use, but accessible in one-spot, and beautiful to use.

We prioritized user stories around these popular planning activities:

  • I want to save a website or article for later
  • I want to create a document
  • I want to organize my items into categories
  • I want to create a spreadsheet
  • I want to share items with other people

WIREFRAMES

Sketches (and then wireframes) were created for all screens needed to complete our prioritized user stories. These initial desktop screens allowed users to first create a project, such as “Wedding, “Bridal Shower” or “Honeymoon”. Within each project, the user could create spreadsheets and documents, upload files, or save links from the web.

USER TESTING

To test these wireframes, we asked participants to complete the following tasks:

  • Add an item
  • Organize an item
  • Share an item

We discovered that the project-based workflow made completing these tasks unintuitive - participants ranked them difficult to complete. In addition, because our language was so generic, users did not immediately see the differentiation between Gather and existing products like Google Drive.

To address these concerns, we removed the project creation step from the workflow and focused all language on wedding planning specifically.

BRANDING

Modern, timeless and gender-neutral were guiding principles for our branding. As a collaborative, productive workspace for couples to plan their wedding, Gather should feel clean and professional, but also inspire creativity. It should appeal to both genders and feel inviting for couples both traditional and non-traditional.

COLORS

We chose a neutral, organic color palette that was heavily white in order to evoke the "blank canvas" on which couples can dream up their personal dream wedding. Since every individual couple has their own unique color palettes and preferences for their event, the neutral color palette would not distract or compete.

TYPE

The type we chose also reflects a modern and timeless aesthetic. Our header font, Playfair Display, is a serif font who lends a serious quality that matches the occassion. It also is reminiscent of type often letterpressed on wedding invitations. For body type, we chose a simple and modern sans-serif to give the site a more productivity-based feel.

PROTOTYPE

We made additional design adjustments based on preference tests and created a high fidelity prototype for a second round of user testing. Participants were asked to complete the following tasks:

  • Add an item
  • Organize an item
  • Share an item

With the wedding planning focus, this prototype tested much better with users. All participants breezed through the tasks and ranked them easy to complete.

For our final product, we addressed feedback on some minor elements that still needed work, such as reorganizing the folder list in the dashboard view.

CLOSING THOUGHTS

We started this project by attempting to create a catch-all project planning product. However, by focusing the use case for wedding planning only, we were able to deliver a tighter product that is more intuitive to use.

In a space as crowded as the wedding industry, wedding planning is still a disorganized experience with few websites stepping up to solve the problem. Gather meets the need for a single destination where modern couples have all the tools to plan their wedding together.
